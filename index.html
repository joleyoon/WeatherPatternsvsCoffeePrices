Overview

This project examines whether broad weather conditions across coffee-growing regions move together with coffee commodity prices. The dashboard combines historical market closes with temperature, humidity, precipitation, and solar radiation data to surface the clearest patterns in the dataset.

Findings

The weather-price relationship is not especially strong at the aggregate daily level, but the dataset still reveals which climate measures move most consistently with price and which growing regions sit in the most extreme conditions.

Weather Correlations

Pearson correlations between daily coffee prices and the market-wide weather averages across all tracked locations.

Currency Context

Exchange rates are included in the dataset and show materially larger correlations with price than any single weather variable.

Forecast

The forecast section uses a simple baseline model: it extends the recent 180-day price trend and wraps the projection with a residual-based uncertainty band. It is useful for presentation and directional storytelling, not for trading decisions.

Method

This page is powered directly from the uploaded CSV. The analysis artifacts are generated once into a static JavaScript file, which means the site can be hosted as plain HTML without any backend.
